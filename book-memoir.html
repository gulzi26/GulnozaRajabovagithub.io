Educated

by Tara Westover

📘 Status: Finished — Happy to discuss 🔖 Read it twice

About this book: A memoir about growing up in a survivalist family in Idaho and the transformative power of education. Westover's journey from homeschooled isolation to university is both harrowing and inspiring.

Why this book matters to me as a teacher

A memoir about education as transformation — read it twice, once for the story and once thinking about my own students' relationship to school. The first time I got swept up in Tara's harrowing childhood and her escape through education. The second time, I kept thinking about what education actually means to people in difficult circumstances, and how powerful it can be.

As a teacher, this book is a powerful reminder of the role we play in our students' lives. For some of them, school isn't just about academics — it's their gateway to a different future. The way Westover describes the power of learning, of being taught to think critically, of having your mind opened — that's what I'm trying to do in my classroom every day.

What stood out to me

  • The profound impact of education on identity and agency
  • How family systems can both enable and prevent growth
  • The courage required to question everything you've been taught
  • The isolation of ignorance versus the freedom of knowledge

Perfect for

  • Educators interested in the transformative power of learning
  • Readers interested in memoir and personal growth
  • Those thinking about identity and family systems
  • Anyone who loves a page-turner that also makes you think
