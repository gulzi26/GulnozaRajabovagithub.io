Pedagogy of the Oppressed

by Paulo Freire

📙 Status: Looking for a copy 🔍 Help me find it!

About this book: A foundational text on critical pedagogy and education as a practice of freedom. Freire argues that education should empower learners rather than treat them as empty vessels to be filled.

Why I need this book

Came up constantly in my Education and Innovation coursework and I never got my own copy. Every time I revisit my notes, I realize how much Freire's ideas shaped my thinking about what teaching should be. If you have one to lend or swap, I'm genuinely interested.

I want to reread it now with my own teaching experience behind me. I'm curious how it will resonate differently after several years in the classroom, and how I can apply his concepts more intentionally with my students.

Why this book matters

  • Challenges traditional banking models of education
  • Emphasizes dialogue and mutual respect in teaching
  • Connects education to social justice and liberation
  • Foundational for critical pedagogy and transformative education

Perfect for

  • Education students and professionals
  • Those interested in social justice
  • Teachers seeking philosophical frameworks
  • Book clubs focused on education or activism
